### Project Description
This project contributes to PhD research investigating the use of virtual reality (VR) to support aviation theory training. Flight training includes both practical and theoretical components. Practical training involves in-aircraft or flight simulator sessions with qualified instructors, while the theoretical component covers subjects such as aerodynamics, meteorology, air law, and navigation. Aviation theory is typically delivered through lecture-based methods, which have been criticised for their limited engagement and effectiveness in promoting knowledge retention. A poor understanding of aviation theory can have serious safety implications, as it impairs decision-making. This is a key factor in pilot error, which contributes to up to 84% of general aviation accidents. 

This project focuses on the development of VR training covering multiple sub-topics from the Australian Commercial Pilot Licence (CPL) aerodynamics syllabus. The VR training is not intended to fully replace formal training but to supplement it by supporting student understanding of aerodynamics concepts that are difficult to visualise. The topics to be covered will be confirmed at the beginning of the project, and there is no expectation that students research aerodynamics theory. Relevant source material with explanations of all theory content will be provided. The content developed will be used in a future comparative study to assess the effectiveness of VR in aviation theory training.

### Deliverables
A functional VR training prototype covering the selected aerodynamics content. The training is intended to be a proof of concept and doesn't need to be fully polished, but it should be engaging, and effective for learning so it can be used for research purposes.
